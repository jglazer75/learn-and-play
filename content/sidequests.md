## **Part 3: Side Quests & Field Trips**

### **Local Quests (Madison, WI)**

1. **Geeks Mania Arcade**  
   * **Location:** Madison, WI  
   * **Mission:** Find the largest physical arcade cabinet (usually the X-Men 6-player cabinet). Play a game of *Pinball* to understand physical mechanics vs. digital.  
2. **I/O Arcade Bar**  
   * **Location:** Williamson St, Madison  
   * **Time Constraint:** Family Hours are **Saturday/Sunday 11am â€“ 2pm**.  
   * **Mission:** Play a round of *Killer Queen* (if available) or a classic cabinet in a social setting.  
3. **Aftershock Classic Arcade**  
   * **Location:** E Washington Ave, Madison  
   * **Time Constraint:** Kids allowed until 7pm.  
   * **Mission:** The "Token" Experience. Teach ACG how to manage a physical budget of tokens.  
4. **Video Game X-Change / Mega Media X-Change**  
   * **Location:** Madison  
   * **Mission:** "The Scavenger Hunt." Go find a physical copy of a game you played on the emulator (e.g., a *Super Mario World* cartridge). Hold history in your hand.

### **Regional Quests (Day Trips)**

5. **Galloping Ghost Arcade**  
   * **Location:** Brookfield, IL (Chicago Metro)  
   * **Significance:** Largest arcade in the USA.  
   * **Mission:** Find the "Rare" aisle. Play a game that was never released on home consoles.  
6. **Midwest Gaming Classic**  
   * **Location:** Milwaukee, WI (Usually in April)  
   * **Significance:** Massive trade show/convention.  
   * **Mission:** Experience a gaming convention atmosphere.

### **National Quest (The Big Trip)**

7. **National Videogame Museum**  
   * **Location:** Frisco, Texas  
   * **Mission:** See the "Pong" console and the 80s bedroom exhibit.  
   * **Why:** It is dedicated specifically to the history you are teaching.  
   * **Alternative:** **The Strong National Museum of Play** (Rochester, NY) \- Includes the World Video Game Hall of Fame.
