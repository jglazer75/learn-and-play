## **Part 2: Reading & Watching List**

### **For the Historian (Dad)**

* **Book:** *Blood, Sweat, and Pixels* by Jason Schreier (Understanding dev cycles).  
* **Book:** *Masters of Doom* by David Kushner (History of PC gaming/Carmack/Romero).  
* **YouTube Channel:** [The Gaming Historian](https://www.youtube.com/user/mcfrosticles) (High production value history docs).  
* **YouTube Channel:** [Ahoy](https://www.youtube.com/user/XboxAhoy) (Deep dives into specific weapons/games, great visuals).  
* **YouTube Channel:** [GDC (Game Developers Conference)](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC0JB7TSe49lg56u6qH8y_MQ) (Specifically "Post-Mortem" talks on classic games).

### **For the Apprentice (7-Year-Old)**

* **TV Series:** *High Score* (Netflix) \- *Note: Watch with Dad; some language/themes in later episodes, but the animations explaining tech are great.*  
* **YouTube Series:** *Boundary Break* (Shesez) \- Shows what happens "off camera" in video games. Great for understanding how games are made.  
* **YouTube:** *The Story of Tetris* (Gaming Historian) \- A clean, fascinating story about the game.
